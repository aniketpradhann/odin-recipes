Butter Chicken

Description

Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a popular North Indian dish known for its creamy tomato-based gravy. Tender chicken pieces are marinated in yogurt and spices, then cooked and simmered in a rich sauce made with butter, tomato puree, cream, and ground cashews.

Ingredients

  • Chicken (boneless, cut into cubes or pieces)
  • Yogurt (plain, preferably Greek yogurt)
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Butter
  • Tomatoes (pureed or finely chopped)
  • Cream (heavy cream or whipping cream)
  • Cashew nuts (ground into a paste)
  • Spices:
    • Cumin powder
    • Coriander powder
    • Garam masala
    • Kashmiri red chili powder (for color)
  • Sugar (optional, to balance flavors)
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves, optional)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Fresh cilantro leaves (for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Marinate the chicken pieces in yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, and a pinch of salt for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat butter in a pan over medium heat.
  3. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are lightly browned and cooked through. Remove the chicken and set aside.
  4. In the same pan, add more butter if needed and saut√© the ginger-garlic paste until fragrant.
  5. Add the tomato puree and cook until the butter starts to separate from the tomato mixture.
  6. Stir in the ground cashew nut paste and cook for a few minutes.
  7. Add cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and Kashmiri red chili powder. Cook the spices for a minute or two.
  8. Return the cooked chicken pieces to the pan and mix well with the sauce.
  9. Stir in cream, sugar (if using), and kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves). Simmer for 5-10 minutes until the sauce thickens to your desired consistency.
  10. Adjust salt to taste.
  11. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.
  12. Serve hot with naan, rice, or roti.
